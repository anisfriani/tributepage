Andrea Hirata

"Bermimpilah, maka Tuhan akan mememluk mimpi-mimpimu." Andrea Hirata, Sang Pemimpi

Andrea Hirata was born with the name of Aqil Barraq Badrudin Seaman Sain Harun (born in October 24, 1967 in Gantung, Belitong) is an Indonesian author who has revolutionized Indonesian literature. While he was young, his parents changed his name seven times. They settled on the name Andrea while the name Hirata was given by his mother. He grew up in a poor family (Seman Said Harunayah and NA Masturah) not far from a goverment-owned mine

Hirata started his tertiary education with a degree in economics from The University of Indonesia. Although major studies taken by Andrea is economy, but he was very fond of science - physics, chemistry, biology, astronomy and literature. After receiving a scholarship from the European Union, he didi his master's degree in Europe. First at the University of Paris then at Sheffield Hallam University, Britain. Andrea more identified himself as an academic and backpacker. Another his dream is to live in Kye Gompa, a village in the Himalaya.

His thesis in economic field of telecommunications was received award from the university and he graduated cum laude. The thesis has been adapted into Indonesian and is the first telecommunication economic theory books written by Indonesia people. The book has been circulated as a scientific reference. Hirata released Laskar Pelangi in 2005. The novel is written in six month based on the experiences of his childhood in the Pacific Islands. Its also spawned three sequel: Sang Pemimpi, Edensor and Maryamah Karpov. He later described it as "an irony about the lack of access to education for the children in the one of the richest islands in the world."
